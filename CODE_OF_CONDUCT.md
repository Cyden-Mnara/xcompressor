# Code of Conduct

The xcompressor community should be useful, respectful, and safe for contributors and users.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and patient with people of different skill levels, languages, backgrounds, and constraints.
- Keep feedback specific, actionable, and focused on the work.
- Credit ideas, fixes, reports, and upstream projects fairly.
- Assume public discussions are visible to users, maintainers, and future contributors.
- Help keep issues and pull requests on topic.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, slurs, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions after maintainers ask for focus.
- Submitting code, media, or documentation that violates licenses or another person's rights.
- Pressuring maintainers or contributors for unpaid work, immediate replies, or private support.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues or pull requests; block users; or take other reasonable action to protect the project. Enforcement should be proportional, documented where practical, and focused on keeping the community healthy.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the project maintainer. If a public issue is unsafe or inappropriate, avoid escalating the thread and contact a maintainer directly.

If you maintain this repository publicly, replace this line with a dedicated reporting email or security contact before announcing the project.
